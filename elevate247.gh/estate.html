BUSINESS REGISTRATION

THREE MAIN FUNCTION

  • (i) Sole Proprietorship Registration

    (ii) Partnership Registration

    (iii) Limited Liability Company Registration

  • Procedures Involved in Business Registration

    (i) Submission of Name Reservation Request for Approval

    (ii) Completion and Submission of the Business Registration Forms

    (iii) Payment of Prescribed Fees and Levies

    (iv) Verification of Details and Issuance of Certificate of Registration or Incorporation

    (v) Currently, we register an average of 200 new businesses monthly, with peak periods around the start of the year.

  • Partnership Registration

    Involves the creation of a formal agreement between two or more persons to run a business together. All partners are jointly responsible for liabilities and profits.

    Partnership agreements must be submitted during registration and should outline roles, profit sharing, and dispute resolution mechanisms.

  • Requirements for Business Registration

    Applicants are required to provide a valid national ID, passport-size photographs, digital address, and details of business location and activity.

    Additionally, registered tax identification numbers (TINs) of all owners or directors are mandatory.

    For the first quarter of 2025 (January to March), over 3,500 businesses were registered, generating approximately GHC 4.8 million in registration fees.
